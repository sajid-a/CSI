Linux Workshop

Linux is a Unix-like computer operating system assembled under the model of free and open source software development and distribution. The defining component of Linux is the Linux kernel, an operating system kernel first released 5 October 1991 by Linus Torvalds.
Linux was originally developed as a free operating system for Intel x86-based personal computers. It has since been ported to more computer hardware platforms than any other operating system. It is a leading operating system on servers and other big iron systems such as mainframe computers and supercomputers: more than 90% of today's 500 fastest supercomputers run some variant of Linux, including the 10 fastest. Linux also runs on embedded systems (devices where the operating system is typically built into the firmware and highly tailored to the system) such as mobile phones, tablet computers, network routers, televisions and video game consoles; the Android system in wide use on mobile devices is built on the Linux kernel. Operating System Used: Linux (CentOS distribution)

Code Wars

The objective of this event was to test the participants on their skills as a programmer. The goal was to find the best individual or a team who had the best knowledge about programming as well as a good programmer. The Event consisted of 2 rounds. The first round was an objective quiz round. In this round the participants were asked to solve 30 questions in 15 minutes. Each question was worth 1 point and there was no negative marking. The top 50% of the participants were qualified to move forward to the second round.

The second round was the programming round. In this round the participants were given a time of 1 hour and 3 questions. 2 of a lower d ifficulty while the third was a comparatively difficult one. The scoring pattern was 5 points for the easier ones and 10 for the difficult question. 3 of the best teams were selected as the winners based on the scores they obtained.
